Voting Process

Steps in Voting

  1. Find out where to vote through COMELEC's Precinct Finder.
  2. Proceed to the Voters' Assistance Desk (VAD) to secure your precinct and sequence numbers and assigned room or clustered precinct.
  3. Go to your assigned room and introduce yourself to the Electoral Board by stating your name, precinct, and sequence numbers.
  4. Get your ballot, ballot secrecy folder, and marking pen, then fill out the ballot at the voting area.
  5. Accomplish the ballot by fully shading the oval appearing before the name of the candidate you wish to vote for. Do not overvote.
  6. Feed the ballot into the Vote Counting Machine (VCM).
  7. Check your voter's receipt and deposit it in the receptacle.
  8. Have your right forefinger nail stained with indelible ink.

For Vulnerable Sectors

Special provisions are in place for senior citizens, PWDs, and pregnant women to ensure a seamless voting experience:

  1. Go to your precinct and look for the priority lane for senior citizens, PWDs, and pregnant women.
  2. Present a valid government ID to the facilitator.
  3. Wait for your turn and cast your vote inside the precinct. You can bring a cheat sheet or a list of the candidates you want to vote for to make the process easier.
  4. Insert your ballot into the Vote Counting Machine (VCM). For senior citizens, persons with disabilities, and illiterate voters, headphones will be provided to follow VCM instructions clearly.
  5. Indelible ink will be applied to your forefinger nail as proof of voting.
  6. Review your voter's receipt and drop it into the receipt receptacle box.
