Who is Eligible for the Upcoming Election?

You can only vote if you are a registered voter in the Philippines.

FOR REGULAR VOTERS

  • Must be at least eighteen (18) years of age on or before election day.
  • Must be a resident of the Philippines for at least one (1) year, and six (6) months in the place where you intend to vote immediately preceding the election.
  • Must not be otherwise disqualified by law.

FOR SK VOTERS

  • Must be at least fifteen (15) years of age but not more than thirty (30) years of age on or before election day.
  • Must be a resident for six (6) months in the place where you intend to vote immediately preceding the election.
  • Must not be otherwise disqualified by law.

Documentary Requirements

You must be able to establish your identity by providing a valid identification document. The following are accepted IDs for registration:

  • Philippine Identification Card (PhilID).
  • Employee’s ID with the signature of the employer or authorized representative.
  • Postal ID.
  • PWD Discount ID.
  • Student’s ID or library card, signed by the school authority.
  • Senior Citizen’s ID.
  • Driver’s license.
  • NBI clearance.
  • Passport.
  • SSS/GSIS ID.
  • Integrated Bar of the Philippines (IBP) ID.
  • License issued by the Professional Regulatory Commission (PRC).
  • Certificate of Confirmation issued by the National Commission on Indigenous Peoples (NCIP) for members of ICCs or IPs.
  • Any other valid ID.

Without any acceptable identification documents, the applicant may be validated under oath by any registered voter from the precinct where they plan to register, or by any of their relatives within the fourth civil degree of consanguinity or affinity. No registered voter or relative is permitted to identify more than three (3) applicants.

Community Tax Certificates (cedula) and PNP clearance will not be accepted as valid identification documents for voter registration.

For more information about voter registration, visit the official website of COMELEC.
